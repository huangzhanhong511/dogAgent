# Dog Depression: Symptoms and How To Help Your Dog

[](/author/dr-brittany-kleszynski)

Dog depression is a change in mood that causes decreased interest in activities, low energy, and withdrawal—and it often signals an underlying medical or emotional issue that needs veterinary attention.

### Key Takeaways

  * Dog depression is a behavioral and emotional change, not a formal medical diagnosis.
  * Common signs include low energy, withdrawal, appetite changes, and altered sleep.
  * Underlying medical issues are a major cause and must be ruled out first by a vet.
  * Environmental changes, anxiety, and boredom may also contribute to a dog's depression.



## What Is Dog Depression?

Dogs experience [a wide range of emotions](https://www.petmd.com/dog/behavior/how-to-read-dog-body-language) throughout their lives—just like people do. These emotions are shaped by life experiences or health concerns that affect how they think, feel, and act.

For example, dogs may become depressed due to an underlying health issue or because of a sudden change in their home, such as the loss of a family member.

Dog depression refers to a noticeable change in mood and behavior that results in reduced engagement, sadness, or withdrawal.

While dog depression is not considered a medical emergency, it should never be ignored. If your otherwise [happy dog](https://www.petmd.com/dog/behavior/is-my-dog-happy) suddenly becomes withdrawn or simply seems unlike himself, it’s important to visit a veterinarian to identify the cause.

If your dog seems depressed and has completely [stopped eating](https://www.petmd.com/dog/symptoms/why-my-dog-not-eating), is [vomiting](https://www.petmd.com/dog/symptoms/vomiting-in-dogs), is partaking in [destructive behaviors](https://www.petmd.com/dog/behavior/how-to-stop-dogs-from-chewing-and-scratching-everything), or is [extremely lethargic](https://www.petmd.com/dog/symptoms/why-my-dog-lethargic), take your pup to an emergency veterinarian.

## Symptoms of Dog Depression

Depressed dogs may display the following symptoms:

  * Decreased interest in activities

  * Sad, droopy eyes

  * Ears pulled back

  * Hiding or withdrawing

  * [Whimpering](https://be.chewy.com/help-my-adult-dogs-whining-is-out-of-control/)

  * [Howling](https://www.petmd.com/dog/behavior/why-do-dogs-howl)

  * [Clinging to pet parent](https://www.petmd.com/dog/conditions/behavioral/separation-anxiety-dogs)

  * Low energy

  * Restlessness

  * Decreased appetite

  * Changes in sleep



## Causes of Dog Depression

Dog depression can develop for a variety of reasons, which can include medical issues, environmental factors, or behavioral concerns.

A major cause of depression is underlying disease.

Dogs who feel unwell, especially those who have chronic health conditions like [diabetes](https://www.petmd.com/dog/conditions/endocrine/diabetes-dogs), may experience a general feeling of sadness.

Dogs who have chronic pain due to conditions such as [arthritis](https://www.petmd.com/dog/conditions/musculoskeletal/osteoarthritis-in-dogs) are also more likely to develop depression.

Changes in the dog’s environment may also contribute to a depressive episode. This may include:

  * Moving homes

  * A [death](https://www.petmd.com/care/do-dogs-and-cats-grieve) in the family

  * A new baby

  * Work schedule changes

  * Family conflict



Sometimes, adding [a new furry family member](https://www.petmd.com/dog/behavior/how-to-introduce-dogs) can leave the first pet feeling depressed.

Dogs who don’t receive enough [mental stimulation](https://www.petmd.com/dog/general-health/ways-to-keep-dog-mentally-stimulated) throughout the day can become bored, which can lead to depression. Most dogs need [regular exercise](https://www.petmd.com/dog/wellness/evr_dg_exercising_with_your_dog101) throughout the day to remain healthy and happy.

Lastly, dogs who are anxious can become [fearful and avoidant](https://www.petmd.com/dog/conditions/behavioral/c_dg_fears_phobia_anxiety).

Possible triggers include:

  * [Storms](https://www.petmd.com/dog/conditions/behavioral/c_dg_thunderstorm_phobias)

  * Strangers

  * [Loud noises](https://www.petmd.com/dog/behavior/scary-sounds-understanding-noise-phobia-dogs)

  * New environments

  * Loneliness



These behavioral changes can lead to depression in dogs.

## How Veterinarians Diagnose Dog Depression

While there’s no simple test to diagnose depression in dogs, veterinarians can speak with the pet parent about what behavioral changes are occurring in their dog and rule out other conditions that may be contributing factors.

A veterinarian may ask the pet parent if there have been any recent changes to the home environment, what concerning symptoms the dog is experiencing, and if the dog has lost interest in activities he used to enjoy.

[A complete physical exam](https://www.petmd.com/general-health/pet-wellness-exams-how-prepare) will be done to identify obvious abnormalities that could indicate a medical condition.

[Full blood work](https://www.petmd.com/dog/procedure/blood-work-for-dogs-and-cats) and [a urinalysis](https://www.petmd.com/procedure/urinalysis-for-cats-and-dogs) can provide the veterinarian with a picture of the dog’s overall health and identify underlying diseases that could be causing depression.

Some dogs may benefit from a consultation with [a veterinary behaviorist](https://www.petmd.com/dog/behavior/do-you-need-hire-dog-behaviorist) who can identify depression and offer guidance on next steps.

## Treatment of Dog Depression

Many dogs with depression quickly feel better once the cause has been identified and treated. Management options for depressed dogs include:

  * **Treat the underlying medical issue:** If the dog is unwell or has a chronic disease, treatment should be focused on the illness. By making the dog feel better, his overall mood should improve.

  * **Manage chronic pain:** Dogs with chronic pain, such as seniors with arthritis, benefit from [pain management](https://www.chewy.com/galliprant-grapiprant-tablets-dogs/dp/172975?&utm_source=petmd.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=dog-conditions&utm_term=pain%20management&utm_content=/dog/conditions/behavioral/dog-depression-symptoms-and-how-help-your-dog) and [joint supplements](https://www.petmd.com/vet-verified/best-joint-supplements-for-dogs) to manage their conditions. Orthopedic dog beds are also beneficial to help cushion their joints.

  * **Increase mental stimulation** : Dogs who are bored and do not receive enough mental stimulation may become depressed. Offering [more walks](https://www.petmd.com/dog/general-health/how-often-should-you-walk-your-dog), [games of fetch](https://www.chewy.com/b/fetch-toys-317?ref-query=dog+fetch+toys&ref=searchRedirect&nav-submit-button=&utm_source=petmd.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=dog-conditions&utm_term=games%20of%20fetch&utm_content=/dog/conditions/behavioral/dog-depression-symptoms-and-how-help-your-dog), and [puzzle toys](https://www.chewy.com/ourpets-sushi-treat-dispensing-puzzle/dp/285400?utm_source=petmd.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=dog-conditions&utm_term=puzzle%20toys&utm_content=/dog/conditions/behavioral/dog-depression-symptoms-and-how-help-your-dog) can help boost a dog’s mood.

  * **Use medical management:** Some dogs may require [anti-anxiety medications](https://www.petmd.com/dog/behavior/10-medications-dog-anxiety), like [fluoxetine](https://www.chewy.com/fluoxetine-generic-capsules-dogs/dp/173518?utm_source=petmd.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=dog-conditions&utm_term=fluoxetine&utm_content=/dog/conditions/behavioral/dog-depression-symptoms-and-how-help-your-dog), to help control their symptoms. Before you decide to begin this type of treatment, consult with a veterinary behaviorist.



## Recovery and Management of Dog Depression

Recovery from dog depression largely depends on the cause.

In many cases, the dog simply needs time. This is especially true for dogs experiencing sudden home changes, such as the loss of a family member or a new baby.

The dog needs time to adjust to his new normal, and the exact amount of time that takes varies greatly among each individual dog.

If the depression was caused by a medical issue, treatment generally resolves a dog’s depression in days or weeks.

However, dogs with chronic conditions may experience recurrent bouts of depression over time that must be managed by you and your vet, sometimes with the added help of a veterinary behaviorist.

It’s important to remember that each individual dog will handle stressors uniquely and may experience depression differently.

Working with a veterinarian is the best way to start your dog on the path to recovery.

## Prevention of Dog Depression

There are steps you can take to reduce the risk of dog depression. Most importantly, ensuring your dog receives regular veterinary care will identify health issues early so they can be treated before depression develops.

Providing your dog with enough mental stimulation throughout the day is important in keeping his mood up and preventing depressive episodes.

[Regular walks](https://www.petmd.com/dog/general-health/dog-walking-tips-how-to-walk-your-dog), [socialization](https://be.chewy.com/dog-socialization-pet-etiquette-tips-for-meeting-new-friends-vs-old-friends/), and [training](https://be.chewy.com/sit-stay-and-beyond-weve-got-the-essential-guide-to-basic-dog-obedience-training/) are helpful.

Managing anxiety and comforting your dog through major life transitions or changes in the home environment is important. All of these changes can be overwhelming for your dog.

If you anticipate a stressful event that may affect your pup, speaking with a veterinarian ahead of time can equip you with the tools needed to best support your furry companion.

## Dog Depression FAQs

### How do you cheer up a depressed dog?

Start with a veterinary visit to rule out illness. Once medical issues are addressed, increased exercise, enrichment, and quality time often help improve mood.

### What activities are good for depressed dogs?

Daily walks, puzzle toys, games of fetch, training sessions, and social interaction can all boost a dog’s emotional well-being.

### How do you get a dog to eat when they’re depressed?

If appetite loss persists, consult your veterinarian. They may recommend dietary changes or, in some cases, medical support.

They may recommend changing the food, adding [lean chicken](https://www.petmd.com/dog/nutrition/can-dogs-eat-chicken), or in some cases, giving [an appetite stimulant](https://www.petmd.com/dog/nutrition/appetite-stimulants-dogs).
